What patient messaging could look like beyond SMS

Doccla already messages patients about kits, vitals and check-ins over SMS. RCS (Rich Communication Services) keeps the same phone number and channel, but adds verified branding, rich cards, quick-reply buttons and two-way structured check-ins — no app download required.

Why it matters

Built for the kind of messages Doccla already sends

Verified sender

A blue checkmark and the Doccla name/logo on every message — patients can trust it's not a phishing text, which matters when you're sending links to upload vitals.

Rich cards, not bare links

Setup guides, appointment confirmations and device alerts render as cards with an image, summary and tappable buttons — instead of a wall of text and a raw URL.

One-tap structured replies

Suggested-reply chips turn a symptom check-in into a tap, not a typed sentence — and the response comes back as clean structured data, not free text to parse.

Delivery & read receipts

Know whether a patient has actually seen a "your readings are overdue" nudge before a care coordinator picks up the phone to chase it manually.

Carousels for guidance

Swipeable cards for things like "how to fit your BP cuff" or "what your readings mean" — reusable content, no separate help-centre link needed.

Graceful SMS fallback

If a patient's handset or carrier doesn't support RCS, the same message degrades to plain SMS automatically — no patient is left without a message.
